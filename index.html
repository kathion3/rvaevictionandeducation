School Accreditation

"Accredited with conditions" indicates that a school has been rated below-state-level performance in one or more category under Virginia accreditation standards. This means at least one standard was rated at Level Three which means the school is performing below the state standard for that indicator. Such indicators include academic achievement in Math, English, and Science, achievement gaps in Math and English, and Chronic Absenteeism (missing 15+ days in a school year).

Visualizing eviction trends and achievement gap level trends with line charts would be effective in visualizing temporal data. Additionally, combining eviction and school quality data and visualizing links between eviction rates in a school zone and chronic absenteeism and graduation rates in those schools would allow us to examine in detail these patterns.
Further, adding in the context of redlining would likely prove to provide a new layer to the analysis. Redlining was the practice of grading neighborhoods A, B, C, or D according to neighborhood quality (which often stood in for race) to mark which neighborhoods would be eligible to receive federal loans for homeownership. Hence, redlining is particularly relevant in the current state of neighborhoods and eviction rates as it decided to whom home ownership and opportunity was accessible.
